My path in software engineering

Learning by building

I study software engineering with a focus on fundamentals: data structures, systems design, and how hardware constraints influence software decisions. I learn best by building small tools and working prototypes that make those ideas tangible.

Graphics and visual systems

I am drawn to graphics because it balances strict technical rigor with visual intuition. Whether it is an OpenGL fractal viewer or a shader experiment, I enjoy the iterative loop between math, code, and visual output.

Building from scratch

I like understanding the primitives behind the tools I use. That means writing small engines, implementing algorithms directly, and paying attention to performance details.

Technical + artistic balance

I appreciate that good software can be both precise and expressive. My goal is to craft systems that are reliable, but also visually clear and pleasant to use.
